// THE PROBLEM

No robot on Earth can walk into an unknown room and figure it out.

Not because the hardware isn't ready. Because the software paradigm is fundamentally wrong.

Every major lab follows the same playbook. One model per task. Months of training. Millions in compute. Hundreds of watts. Each skill frozen the moment training ends. $20 billion poured into this approach. Zero general-purpose robots shipping at scale.

This is not a scaling problem. It is an architecture problem. You cannot brute-force infinity.

The industry is trying to solve infinity with spreadsheets. We need a brain.

// THE PROOF

The proof that another way exists has been walking among us for 300,000 years.

The human brain runs on 20 watts. It controls 600+ muscles with sub-millimeter precision. It processes vision, touch, balance, and sound in parallel. It learns continuously for 80 years without forgetting how to walk. It picks up any new tool in minutes. No dataset. No GPU cluster. No reward function.

A two-year-old who has never seen a broom can grab it and push it across the floor. Not because she was trained on 10,000 videos of sweeping, but because she has an internal model of the world rich enough to improvise. She needs 30 seconds of exploration.

This is the intelligence the world needs in its robots. And the entire industry is looking the other way.

// THE ARCHITECTURE

We don't train models. We build brains.

Our system is biomimetic and hierarchical. Modeled after the only structure that actually solves general-purpose physical intelligence: the biological brain.

Spinal Cord
Reflexes & postural correction
Fast, hardwired. No learning needed.
Cerebellum
Internal body model
Predicts physical consequences of actions via forward simulation.
Cortex
Predictive coding & world model
Learns online, minimizes prediction error (Free Energy).
Hippocampus
Episodic memory
Stores past, retrieves by similarity. "This saved me last time."
Basal Ganglia
Action selection & planning
Evaluates candidate sequences. Chooses the safest trajectory.
Active Inference
Unifying principle
Predict, act, correct. Continuously. (Friston, 2010)

No backpropagation. No training phase. No task-specific architecture. No GPU required. The entire brain runs at 1000 Hz on a laptop CPU.

Drop it into a humanoid and it discovers how to balance. A quadruped and it learns to move. A body with 17 joints that has never existed in nature — and it figures it out. No retraining. No fine-tuning. The same brain.
