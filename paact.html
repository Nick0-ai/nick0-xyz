PAACT

Bet real money on your goals.

01 · Origin

How PAACT was born

PAACT started at Innov Hack Paris in January 2026. Nicolas Philippe and his team of four won first place among 150 participants in a 10 hour nonstop hackathon. The concept was too good to leave as a prototype, so Nick0 went home and built the full iOS app in seven days.

The idea is simple: people fail their goals because there are no real consequences. What if quitting actually cost you money?

02 · Mechanism

How it works

PAACT runs on commitment contracts. You set a goal, put real money on it, and pick a deadline. Hit the goal, keep your money. Fail, you pay. No extensions, no excuses. The psychology behind it is loss aversion: people are roughly twice as motivated to avoid losing something as they are to gain something of equal value. PAACT turns that wiring into a productivity tool. Instead of fighting human nature, it uses it.

03 · Features

What you can do

Every screen serves one loop: set a goal, commit money, track progress, hit the deadline or pay.

  • Goal creation Set clear, measurable goals with specific deadlines. No vague intentions, only concrete targets.
  • Financial stakes Commit real money to each goal. You choose the amount, and the stakes are real.
  • Progress tracking Visual progress indicators so you always know where you stand relative to your deadline.
  • Deadline management Automated deadline tracking with notifications as your commitment date approaches.
  • Clean minimal interface Every screen is stripped to its essentials. No clutter, no gamification gimmicks, just your goals and your money on the line.
04 · Technical

How it was built

Nick0 had never written a line of Swift before this project. Picked up the language, learned SwiftUI's declarative paradigm, and shipped a complete iOS app in seven days. All design, from information architecture to individual screens, done from scratch. No templates, no design kits.

Data persistence uses Core Data, keeping everything on device. Solo project start to finish: designed, coded, tested, and submitted by one person.
