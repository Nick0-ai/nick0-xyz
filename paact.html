01 — Origin

How PAACT was born

PAACT was born at Innov Hack Paris in January 2026, where Nicolas Philippe and his team of four won first place among 150 participants in a 10 hour non stop hackathon. The energy in that room was electric, and the concept that emerged from it was too good to leave as a prototype. After the win, Nicolas went home and built the full iOS app in just seven days. Seven days from hackathon demo to a complete, polished product ready for the App Store.

The concept came from a simple observation that anyone who has ever set a New Year's resolution will recognize immediately. People set goals all the time and fail because there are no real consequences. Motivation fades, accountability is vague, and the only thing you lose when you quit a goal is a bit of self respect, which most people have learned to live without. The question that sparked PAACT was straightforward: what if failing your goals actually cost you money?

02 — Mechanism

How it works

PAACT is built on the principle of commitment contracts, a concept grounded in decades of behavioral economics research. The mechanism is elegant in its simplicity. When you set a goal, you commit real money to it. If you achieve the goal by the deadline, you keep your money. If you fail, you pay. There is no wiggle room, no extensions, no excuses. The contract is binary: you either did it or you did not.

The psychological principle at work is loss aversion, one of the most robust findings in behavioral science. Humans are roughly twice as motivated to avoid losing something as they are to gain something of equivalent value. A twenty dollar loss feels about as painful as a forty dollar gain feels good. PAACT turns that deeply wired psychological bias into a practical productivity tool. Instead of fighting human nature, it leverages it.

The closest comparable product is Beeminder, which has been running commitment contracts for years with a devoted user base. PAACT takes the same core idea but wraps it in a much cleaner, more modern user experience designed specifically for mobile. Where Beeminder can feel complex and data heavy, PAACT aims to be something you set up in thirty seconds and forget about until your deadline approaches.

03 — Features

What you can do

PAACT ships with a focused feature set. Every screen serves the core loop: set a goal, commit money, track progress, hit the deadline or pay. Nothing extraneous, nothing that distracts from the commitment.

  • Goal creation Set clear, measurable goals with specific deadlines. No vague intentions, only concrete targets.
  • Financial stakes Commit real money to each goal. You choose the amount, and the stakes are real.
  • Progress tracking Visual progress indicators so you always know where you stand relative to your deadline.
  • Deadline management Automated deadline tracking with notifications as your commitment date approaches.
  • Clean minimal interface Every screen is stripped to its essentials. No clutter, no gamification gimmicks, just your goals and your money on the line.
04 — Technical

How it was built

PAACT was built entirely in Swift and SwiftUI for iOS. Nicolas Philippe had never written a line of Swift before this project. He picked up the language, learned SwiftUI's declarative paradigm, and shipped a complete app in seven days. The entire design, from the information architecture down to individual screen layouts, was done by Nicolas as well. No templates, no design kits, no outsourced assets.

Data persistence uses Core Data for local storage, keeping everything on device. The app is currently in App Store submission, going through Apple's review process. There is no Android version planned yet; the focus is on getting the iOS experience exactly right before considering other platforms.

PAACT is a solo project through and through. One person designed it, coded it, tested it, and submitted it.
